# SOST public-wording legal/regulatory audit

Goal: keep all public SOST wording legally cautious — describe what SOST **is**
(a native Layer-1 PoW blockchain with fixed supply, open-source infrastructure,
on-chain verification tools, PoPC, ConvergenceX mining, and an *observable* Gold
Vault funding mechanism) and avoid wording that frames it as money, currency,
legal tender, a payment instrument, an e-money token, a stablecoin, a redeemable
gold-backed token, a guaranteed store of value, or a product promising returns.

This is an internal risk review, **not legal advice**. A qualified lawyer should
sign off before launch/marketing.

## D. RECOMMEND your decision (core-narrative terms — not auto-changed)
These are deeper brand/economic-model terms. Removing them is a positioning decision
I did not make unilaterally. My recommendation:

1. **"Store of Value" (Pillar 4)** — index.html + whitepaper present a 4th pillar named
   "STORE OF VALUE". "Store of value" carries an investment/value-preservation connotation
   (regulatory + advertising risk). **Recommended:** rename the pillar to **"GOLD RESERVE"**
   or **"RESERVE DISCIPLINE"** (keeps the technical meaning — Pillar 4 is the gold-reserve
   funding mechanism — without the loaded term). Confirm and I'll apply across both pages.
2. **"sound money"** — whitepaper ("SOST treats «sound money» as four orthogonal properties")
   and btctalk-ann (contrasting gold custody). It's a Bitcoin-style monetary-theory framing.
   **Recommended:** reframe to **"sound-asset / monetary-grade scarcity properties"** or
   scope it explicitly as design philosophy, not a claim that SOST *is* money. Confirm to apply.

## Standing rule
Do not use "money", "currency", "legal tender", "payment instrument", "guaranteed reserve",
"redeemable", "backed by gold", "price floor", "stable value", "investment return", "yield",
"profit", "security token" or similar anywhere **unless explicitly framed as what SOST is NOT.**
Gold Vault: use "observable reserve mechanism", "Gold Vault funding mechanism", "on-chain
transparency", "no redemption promise", "not a peg", "no price floor", "no guarantee of value".
