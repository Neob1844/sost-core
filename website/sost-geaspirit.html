RESEARCH:

We downloaded a FREE satellite photo of a real mining region in Chile. We taught an AI to recognize mineral signatures in the image using known mine locations as training data. Result: the AI correctly identifies 96% of known mines and is right 88% of the time when it says "dig here". This was done with $0 budget, public satellite data, and ~10 minutes of computation.
MINERAL INDICES COMPUTED
Iron Oxide (B4/B2)Detects rust-like minerals. High values suggest gossans (oxidized ore caps) or iron-rich rocks.
Clay/Hydroxyl (B11/B12)Detects clay minerals formed by hydrothermal alteration — a key indicator of buried mineralization.
Ferrous Iron (B11/B8A)Detects ferrous iron minerals. Different from rust — indicates fresh/unweathered iron-bearing rock.
Laterite (B4/B3)Detects lateritic weathering profiles. Common in tropical/subtropical mineral-rich terrains.
NDVIVegetation index. Used as a mask — bare ground is where mineral detection works best.
