Position Market

SOST Position Market

A signed market infrastructure for identified positions and rights linked to tokenized gold, with private coordination, verifiable settlement, and full auditability.

LIMITED ALPHA

1. Overview

SOST is developing a signed market infrastructure for identified positions and contractual rights linked to tokenized gold, precious metals, and Proof of Personal Custody structures.

The system is not designed as a traditional AMM. Its core tradeable objects are not generic pool-based fungible assets, but identified positions with specific backing, maturity, and rights configurations.

The market is therefore being built as a signed OTC/RFQ-style venue for positions and rights, with private coordination, verifiable settlement, and full auditability.

Current status: The system operates as a limited alpha. It is operator-assisted, participant-restricted, and not an open public trading venue.

2. What the System Is Designed to Trade

The SOST market is designed to support the exchange of identified positions, rather than generic pool tokens.

Model B Positions

Model B positions are backed through an escrow structure. These are the most straightforward positions to register, value, transfer, and settle.

Full Position Transfer

A participant transfers the entire identified position to another participant. Ownership, backing, and reward all transfer together.

Reward-Right Transfer

A participant keeps the principal position but transfers only the right to the future reward. The buyer gets a child position (reward only).

Model A Rights

Model A is based on personal custody rather than escrow. Because the custody obligation is more closely linked to the original holder, Model A does not enter the market in the same way as Model B.

The intended path is:

  • First: reward rights or identified claims
  • Later, where appropriate: controlled novation or reassignment paths

3. How a Trade Works

A trade is based on signed private coordination and deterministic settlement logic:

1A seller creates a signed offer (ED25519 signature, canonical hash, unique nonce, expiry)
2A buyer responds with a signed acceptance
3The system derives a deterministic deal_id
4The settlement layer verifies: ownership, position status, expiry, signature validity, nonce uniqueness
5If all checks pass: full position transfer or reward-right split is executed
6A signed settlement notice is generated
7The complete event trail is recorded for auditability

The system is designed around identified contractual objects, not anonymous pool interactions.

4. Why a Position Market Exists

Different participants value the same position differently.

Sellers may prefer
  • Immediate liquidity
  • Earlier exit
  • Reduced waiting time
  • Reduced operational burden
  • Selling only the reward, keeping the principal
Buyers may prefer
  • Discounted entry
  • Exposure to future value
  • Access to existing positions without originating from scratch
  • Portfolio construction across maturities

The market exists because future value and present liquidity are not valued equally by all participants.

5. Position Structure

Within the SOST architecture, a position is an identified registry object linked to:

  • A specific backing structure (escrow or autocustody)
  • A maturity or lock profile
  • A defined reward configuration
  • A current owner
  • An auditable lifecycle

The objective is to build a market for identified positions and rights, not a broad public fungible token market.

7. Regulatory and Operational Posture

Any broader public market phase would be subject to legal and operational review.

Under the EU Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA), operating a public trading platform for crypto-assets can trigger significant obligations around operating rules, system resilience, transparency of trading information, order handling, data retention, and settlement timing.

MiCA also excludes crypto-assets that qualify as financial instruments under existing financial law, which means some structures may require a different legal analysis depending on their design.

For that reason: the current system should be understood as a limited alpha position market, not as an unrestricted public exchange.
