[size=14pt][b]SOST — PoPC Redesigned: One Native Bond, Gold as a Boost[/b][/size]
[i]Sovereign Stock Token[/i][/center]

[hr]

[size=12pt][b]TL;DR[/b][/size]
Proof of Personal Custody (PoPC) has been redesigned from a two-model system into a [b]single, SOST-native protocol[/b]. There is now [b]one bond, posted in SOST[/b] — the only collateral and the only thing that can be slashed. Gold (XAUT/PAXG) becomes a [b]moderate reward boost[/b], never collateral and never slashable. No external blockchain sits in the root of security anymore.

[hr]

[size=12pt][b]Why we changed it[/b][/size]

The previous design had two models:
[list]
[li][b]Model A[/b] — native SOST bond, slashable by consensus.[/li]
[li][b]Model B[/b] — gold-vault escrow on Ethereum (XAUT/PAXG).[/li]
[/list]

The problem is simple and structural:

[quote]Security that can be slashed must live where the protocol can slash it.[/quote]

When the bond lived as gold on an external chain, enforcing a penalty required an off-chain watcher, oracle or signing key — a [b]trusted intermediary[/b]. That contradicts the exact property PoPC is meant to guarantee: native, automatic, judge-free enforcement.

Anchoring the bond in SOST removes that dependency:
[list]
[li][b]No external chain as the root of security.[/b][/li]
[li][b]Maximum decentralization[/b] — no operator holds a key that can release or seize anyone's bond.[/li]
[li][b]Maximum automation[/b] — the full lifecycle (lock → audit → reward → slash) runs under SOST consensus, with no human or off-chain component in the trust path.[/li]
[/list]

[hr]

[size=12pt][b]The unified model[/b][/size]

[list]
[li]Every participant posts a [b]single bond in native SOST[/b]. This is the only collateral and the only thing that can be slashed.[/li]
[li]Gold (held in the user's own wallet) is [b]optional[/b]. When present and verified it pays an extra reward on top of the base, funded from a [b]dedicated reserve[/b] so it never dilutes the base reward.[/li]
[li]Gold is [b]never seized[/b]: withdraw your gold, or if verification is temporarily unavailable, you simply revert to the base reward — no penalty, no slash.[/li]
[/list]

[b]Base reward (native SOST bond), by lock duration:[/b]
[code]
Lock duration   1mo    3mo    6mo    9mo    12mo
Base reward      1%     4%     9%    14%     20%
[/code]

[b]Gold Boost — applied on top of the base reward:[/b]
[code]
Gold verified for   0-30 days   31-90 days   91+ days
Boost on base          +0%        +10%        +20%
[/code]

[list]
[li]Cap: [b]+20% operationally[/b], with a [b]+25% technical maximum[/b] reserved for future governance.[/li]
[li]The boost is a multiplier on the base reward, not on the bond.[/li]
[li]Eligibility: tokenized gold worth at least [b]max(25% of the bond value, 0.25 PAXG/XAUT)[/b], held continuously for the verified period. Dust (0.001 PAXG) does not qualify.[/li]
[li]Recommended minimum lock: [b]90 days[/b].[/li]
[/list]

[b]Worked example:[/b] a 12-month native bond earns 20% base. With gold verified for 91+ days, the boost is +20%, so the total is 20% × 1.20 = [b]24%[/b]. The same bond without gold simply earns its 20%.

[hr]

[size=12pt][b]Funding[/b][/size]

The Gold Boost is funded from the [b]PoPC Pool[/b] and capped so it cannot dilute the base PoPC reward. The Metals Protocol Reserve is [b]untouched[/b]. The coinbase split is unchanged:

[code]
Recipient                              Share
Miner                                   50%
Metals Protocol Reserve (Gold Vault)    25%
PoPC Pool (base + optional Gold Boost)  25%
[/code]

Base reward has absolute priority; the boost is paid only from PoPC Pool surplus and can never reduce the base.
